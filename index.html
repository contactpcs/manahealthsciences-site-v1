The opportunity

Why a platform
company. Why now.

Specialised healthcare is growing faster than the infrastructure to deliver it. The gap between what patients need and what the market can consistently provide is wide — and it is precisely that gap that a platform model is positioned to close.

View the investment case

Fragmented markets, structural infrastructure gap

Across India and Europe, specialised healthcare delivery remains fragmented, geographically uneven, and difficult to scale. The clinical capability often exists — what is missing is the operational infrastructure to distribute it consistently. That is the problem Mana is built to solve.

Platform economics compound over time

A shared infrastructure model means that the cost of launching each new brand, clinic, or geography decreases as the platform matures. Technology, protocols, supplier relationships, regulatory learnings, and operational systems are built once and deployed many times — creating durable competitive advantage at scale.

Asset-light, franchise-ready from the ground up

The Mana model is designed to grow without proportional capital intensity. Our technology layer standardises what happens inside every operation in our network — enabling partners, hospitals, and franchise operators to deliver consistent quality under our brands without building systems from scratch.

Indian origin as a strategic foundation

Mana Health Sciences was built in India for global markets. India offers cost-efficient operations, deep clinical talent, and a patient population large enough to generate meaningful outcomes data at speed. We are not entering India as an afterthought — it is our proof of concept and our operational core.
