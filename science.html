Non-invasive brain stimulation is not an experimental category. It is a mature, peer-reviewed field with decades of published research, regulatory approvals in multiple jurisdictions, and a growing body of clinical evidence across neurological and psychiatric indications. This page presents the science for a clinical and investor audience — not for patients.

The evidence base

Not experimental.
Under-distributed.

The peer-reviewed literature on transcranial brain stimulation spans tens of thousands of published studies and multiple decades of controlled clinical research. TMS holds regulatory approval for the treatment of major depressive disorder in the United States, Europe, and several other jurisdictions. tDCS and TPS are backed by a growing evidence base across neurological rehabilitation, pain management, and psychiatric care.

The gap is not scientific confidence — it is infrastructure. The therapies work in clinical trials because trials have protocol discipline. What Mana's platform does is bring that protocol discipline into scalable, franchise-ready clinical operations, making the real-world outcomes more consistent with the trial record.

Our modalities

Three modalities. One platform.

Our clinical network deploys three established brain stimulation modalities, selected and sequenced through our protocol framework based on the clinical profile of each patient and each indication. This is a clinical and investor summary — full clinical detail is available to qualified partners under appropriate terms.

tDCS
Non-invasive
Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation

tDCS

tDCS delivers a low-level, constant electrical current to targeted cortical regions via surface electrodes. It modulates neuronal excitability — increasing or decreasing baseline firing rates depending on stimulation polarity — without triggering action potentials directly. Its safety profile is well established, and the evidence base spans neurological rehabilitation, mood disorders, and chronic pain.

Mood disorder and depression adjunct protocols
Chronic pain — central sensitisation modulation
Cognitive rehabilitation in neurological settings
Highly portable and cost-effective at scale
TMS
Regulatory approved
Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation

TMS

TMS uses rapidly alternating magnetic fields to induce electrical currents in specific cortical regions, depolarising neurons and modulating circuit activity. It is the most evidence-rich of the three modalities and holds regulatory approval for major depressive disorder in the United States and Europe among other jurisdictions. Repetitive TMS (rTMS) is the standard clinical delivery form.

Regulatory approval for treatment-resistant depression
OCD and anxiety disorder protocols
Motor cortex rehabilitation post-stroke
30+ years of published clinical research
TPS
Frontier modality
Transcranial Pulse Stimulation

TPS

TPS uses focused ultrasound pulses to stimulate deep brain structures non-invasively — structures that are beyond the reach of surface-level electrical or magnetic stimulation. Its ability to penetrate deeper cortical and subcortical regions makes it particularly promising in neurological rehabilitation, Alzheimer's research, and indications involving subcortical circuitry.

Deep brain structure access — non-invasive
Neurological rehabilitation and cognitive decline
Active research area with growing evidence base
CE-marked for clinical use in Europe

TMS is the most evidence-rich modality, with decades of published research and regulatory approval for major depressive disorder in multiple jurisdictions. tDCS has a substantial and growing evidence base across neurological and psychiatric applications, with particular strength in rehabilitation and pain management. TPS is the newest of the three modalities and represents a frontier area with active research — its CE-marking for clinical use in Europe reflects regulatory confidence in its safety profile.
All three modalities are non-invasive, drug-free, and have well-characterised safety profiles in the published literature. TMS has been in active clinical use for over 30 years; its adverse event rate is low and typically mild (transient headache, scalp discomfort). tDCS's most commonly reported effects are mild tingling and skin redness under electrodes. TPS, as an ultrasound-based modality, has a clean safety profile in published trials. All sessions in our network are delivered under direct clinical supervision with contraindication screening at intake.
Our technology layer translates the published evidence into structured clinical protocols. Each protocol specifies stimulation parameters, session frequency, contraindication criteria, and outcome measurement methods, drawing on the peer-reviewed literature and the clinical expertise of our protocol development team. The platform then delivers these protocols consistently across our network and captures outcomes data to refine them over time.
TMS holds FDA clearance in the United States for major depressive disorder and certain other indications. TPS holds CE marking in Europe. tDCS is widely used in clinical research and in clinical practice; its regulatory status varies by jurisdiction. Our clinical governance framework ensures that all therapies are delivered in compliance with the applicable regulatory requirements in each market in which we operate.
