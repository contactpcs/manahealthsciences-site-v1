The healthcare platform
infrastructure play.

Mana Health Sciences is building the shared infrastructure — technology, standards, franchise systems — that allows specialised healthcare brands to scale across underserved markets. The model is asset-light. The platform is live. The opportunity is structural.

The investment case

Why Mana.
Why now.

Specialised healthcare is expanding faster than the systems to deliver it. Across emerging and developed markets alike, the bottleneck is not clinical capability — it is infrastructure. Mana was built to be that infrastructure layer: owning the IP, running the systems, and holding equity in the brands that scale on top of it.

Structural infrastructure gap. India and Europe together represent significant unmet demand for standardised, high-quality specialised care. The opportunity exists because consistent infrastructure has not — until now.
Asset-light, franchise-ready model. Our platform and franchise architecture enables geographic expansion without proportional capital intensity. We build the systems once — partners deploy them many times.
Multi-brand platform design. The Mana platform is not built around a single clinical vertical. It is designed to support multiple healthcare brands and specialties — each running on shared infrastructure, IP, and operational standards.
Proof of concept already live. Anava Clinics, our first sub-holding, is operational in India — validating the platform model with real clinical operations, real outcomes data, and a live franchise architecture.
Indian operational foundation. Built with Indian clinical talent and infrastructure — cost-efficient, scalable, and positioned in one of the world's fastest-growing healthcare economies.

The timing argument

The window is open.

Three structural conditions make this the right moment for a platform play in specialised healthcare: the demand has matured, the regulatory environment is increasingly favourable in our target markets, and the infrastructure gap remains large and largely unaddressed.

Condition 01

Demand maturity

Demand for high-quality, standardised specialised healthcare is no longer nascent. Patient awareness, willingness to pay, and clinical referral networks are all reaching a level of maturity that supports confident platform deployment.

Condition 02

Regulatory momentum

Regulatory frameworks in India and Europe are evolving in a direction that is increasingly supportive of platform-based, technology-enabled healthcare delivery. This creates a window for establishing market position before the landscape becomes crowded.

Condition 03

No dominant platform yet

In our target markets, no single entity has established a dominant, franchise-ready platform for specialised healthcare delivery. The gap is real and the window for category definition is open. This is what Mana was built to capture.
