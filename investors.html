The neurotechnology
infrastructure play.

Mana Health Sciences is building the platform infrastructure for the non-invasive neuromodulation category across two of the world's most underserved and fastest-growing healthcare markets. The market opportunity is significant. The evidence base is established. The infrastructure gap is real.

The investment case

Why Mana.
Why now.

Non-invasive brain stimulation therapies are no longer experimental. They are peer-reviewed, regulatory-approved in key jurisdictions, and increasingly recognised as first-line and adjunct treatments across neurological and psychiatric indications. The market is growing. The bottleneck is infrastructure — and infrastructure is exactly what Mana is building.

Large, underserved markets. India and Europe together represent significant unmet demand for advanced neurological and psychiatric care. Access to neuromodulation therapies is fragmented and inconsistent in both markets.
Asset-light, scalable model. Our franchise and platform model enables geographic expansion without proportional capital intensity. The technology layer does the heavy lifting of quality assurance at scale.
Established evidence base. We are not funding research to validate the category. The clinical evidence exists. We are building the infrastructure to distribute it consistently.
Live clinical network. Anava Clinics is operational in India, generating real-world outcomes data and demonstrating the franchise model in practice.
Indian operational foundation. Built in India, with Indian clinical talent and infrastructure. Cost-efficient, scalable, and positioned in a market with strong demand growth trajectory.

The timing argument

The window is open.

Three conditions make this the right moment for a platform play in non-invasive neuromodulation: the evidence has matured, the regulatory environment is increasingly favourable, and the infrastructure gap remains large.

Condition 01

Evidence maturity

The peer-reviewed evidence base for tDCS, TMS, and TPS has reached a level of maturity that supports confident clinical deployment. We are past the research phase — this is an infrastructure and distribution challenge now.

Condition 02

Regulatory trajectory

Regulatory frameworks in both India and Europe are moving in a direction that is increasingly supportive of non-invasive neuromodulation. This creates a window for establishing market position before the landscape becomes crowded.

Condition 03

Infrastructure gap

Despite strong demand and an established evidence base, the infrastructure for consistent, standardised neuromodulation delivery does not yet exist at scale in our target markets. This is the gap Mana was built to fill.
